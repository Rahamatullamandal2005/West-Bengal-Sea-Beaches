Digha

Describe Digha Shortly

Digha, located in the Purba Medinipur district of West Bengal, is a coastal gem that attracts tourists with its serene beauty and vibrant ambiance. Known as the "Brighton of the East," Digha boasts an expansive 7 km beach along the Bay of Bengal, split into Old Digha and New Digha. Old Digha is renowned for its casuarina-lined shores, offering a picturesque setting for leisurely walks and stunning sunsets, while New Digha, with its wider and more spacious beach, provides a perfect spot for swimming and water sports. Visitors can explore attractions like the Marine Aquarium and Regional Centre, Amarabati Park, and the ancient Chandaneswar Temple. The local markets in Digha are bustling with activity, offering a variety of sea shells, handmade crafts, and local delicacies like "Mihidana" and "Sitabhog." Fresh seafood is a culinary highlight, with beachside shacks serving delicious crab curry, prawn malai curry, and various fish preparations. Easily accessible by road and rail, Digha makes for an ideal weekend getaway, especially between October and February when the weather is most pleasant. With its blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and vibrant local life, Digha offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
